## Editing notes (apply to ALL reels)

1. **First 1.5s is sacred.** Open with the most striking visual frame from the hook scene. No text card. No logo. Just the image + the first 5 words of voiceover.
2. **Captions:** Generate auto-captions in CapCut. Use Montserrat Bold 60pt, white with 4px black stroke, positioned at 60% screen height (above the thumb zone).
3. **Cuts:** Match-cut on motion when possible. If a scene ends with Bob looking right, start the next with motion entering from the right.
4. **Music ducking:** Drop music to -18dB under voiceover, restore to -6dB during scene transitions.
5. **Closing card (last 1s):** Black frame, white text: *"Bob Campana — Author. Builder. Pilot."* (or whatever the client's actual positioning is). Soft fade.
6. **Sound design:** Add ambient sound under each scene (CapCut sound library: "Restaurant ambient" for Reel 1, "Helicopter rotor" for Reel 3, etc.) at -24dB. Adds production polish for almost no effort.

## Backup plan if a scene fails to generate

If a specific scene's video keeps producing visual artifacts (melted faces, wrong limbs, bad text), do this instead:

1. Generate the still image only (much more reliable in Gemini/ChatGPT)
2. In CapCut, apply "Ken Burns" effect: slow zoom-in over the still
3. Add a subtle parallax filter
4. Layer voiceover on top

A great still with a slow zoom is better than a janky generated clip. The brief explicitly says: *"Retry, switch tools, refine prompts, edit heavily — the final video is what matters most."*
